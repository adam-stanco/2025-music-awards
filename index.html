2025 Music Awards

Adam Stanco

2025 was a surprisingly blessed year for music. I spent the year listening casually without fixating on one 'golden album,' but looking back, the depth of quality was incredible. I had to cut far more than I expected to reach this final list.

Albums of the Year

01
Sleep Token

Even in Arcadia

They leaned into their magic. Following their breakout success, this feels purpose-built to amplify the atmospheric technicality that made them famous. It's a deep, intentional record that proves they are only getting better.

Listen on Spotify
02
Various Artists

Kimetsu No Yaiba (Demon Slayer) OST

My workout partner for the last nine months. The genius of this OST is the character leitmotifs; hearing individual themes weave together and evolve during battle sequences provides a sense of progression that most albums can't match.

Listen on Spotify
03
Lorien Testard

Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 OST

It isn't hyperbole: this is the greatest video game soundtrack ever made. It's a staggering blend of progressive classical, funk, jazz, and metal: a rare score that is technically complex yet remains deeply emotionally resonant.

Listen on Spotify
04
Dreamwake

The Lost Years

A hidden gem out of Connecticut. Dreamwake has perfectly bridged the gap between heavy metalcore and retro synthwave. It's a niche aesthetic executed with moments that feel genuinely special and cinematic.

Listen on Spotify
05
Three Days Grace

Alienation

The ultimate 'Beautiful Mid' album. It's unapologetic, satisfying rock. The real draw is the return of OG frontman Adam Gontier; hearing him trade vocals with Matt Walst on tracks built for their dual-threat dynamic is a treat for long-time fans.

Listen on Spotify
